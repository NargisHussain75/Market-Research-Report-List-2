<p><p>The marine sewage treatment system market is primarily divided into two types: black water and grey water. Black water refers to wastewater containing human waste from toilets, requiring advanced treatment to eliminate pathogens and contaminants before discharge. In contrast, grey water consists of wastewater from sinks, showers, and laundry, which typically contains fewer pollutants and can be treated using less intensive methods. Both segments are essential for maintaining marine environmental health and ensuring compliance with regulations on waste disposal at sea.</p></p>
