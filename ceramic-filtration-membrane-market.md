<p><p>Ceramic filtration membranes are classified into three main types based on pore size: ultrafiltration, microfiltration, and nanofiltration. Ultrafiltration membranes have larger pores, removing suspended solids and bacteria, while microfiltration membranes are even coarser, effectively filtering out larger particles. Nanofiltration membranes contain smaller pores that can separate divalent ions and larger molecules. Each type serves distinct applications in water treatment, industrial processes, and food and beverage production, capitalizing on their specific filtration capabilities and efficiencies.</p></p>
